Dutch design week

Our project is a short 2-minute video inspired by the Dutch Design Week. It showcases a striking contradiction: despite many artists promoting sustainability, most still use paper business cards. We highlight this by creating a tree out of these collected business cards and then throwing them in the trash, where they all end up anyway. The video's story is about how artists say they care about the environment but use things that harm it. By throwing the cards in the trash, we show that these cards, meant to represent creativity, just become waste. We want artists and viewers to think about using more eco-friendly stuff to promote their work. Our short video aims to make people think about how what we say about the environment should match what we do. It hopes to start a conversation about using more eco-friendly ways to promote art, pushing for better practices in the artistic community.

Day 1

We started this day with a short introduction of the event and meeting the Toronto student Aisha. We then had to come up with a planning for the following three days. We started by choosing which ‘Dutch Design Week – Theme’ we would like to focus on. We have chosen for theme 9: Signature and collectible design. This theme primarily focusses on the individual expression of the artist, translated into a design or object. This theme spoke to us because we think it is important you can express your ideas, emotions, and values through art. This will make you think about what you will contribute and makes you strive to be unique. After choosing a theme, we looked at the DDW-map and create a route. A couple of activities on the route relates to our theme, some activities were an extra we would like to see. Most of the activities were in the city centrum of Eindhoven, making use of the nice weather The Tuesday activities are mostly in Strijp-s.
