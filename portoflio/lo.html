User interaction (analysis and advice)

You analyse the user, the user interaction and the user experience, also taking state of the art interactive technologies into account. You select a suitable design process to be able to advise on UX interventions based on a validated UX design

This learning outcome was proven by:

For the social sphere project, I conducted a comprehensive analysis of our target users, specifically the youth workers in Eindhoven. This involved a deep dive into their needs, preferences, and challenges within the digital landscape. The creation of personas and the tailored content for different types of youth workers based on their personality type made with the archetype quiz.

For the dutch design week, Identifying the contradiction and proposing a visual representation demonstrated an understanding of user perceptions and behaviors.

In the creation of my portfolio website for the advanced media design semester, my focus on user interaction was integral to addressing the main research question: "How can the integration of interactive and aesthetic aspects, and AI technologies enhance the user experience on a website?"

For the planet project,The design of the travel poster involved analyzing user preferences to ensure its effectiveness in attracting Earth inhabitants.

User interaction (execution & validation)

You execute and evaluate the user experience of an interactive product. You document the development process for the stakeholders.

This learning outcome was proven by:

As part of the social sphere project, executing and validating the user experience, I actively engaged in the practical aspects of bringing our interactive product to life. This included hands-on involvement in the development process and the subsequent evaluation of the user experience. Through meticulous documentation, I provided stakeholders with insights into the developmental journey, ensuring transparency and accountability in the execution of the project.

Also with the contribution to the creation of the Figma prototype and conducted usability testing to ensure a positive user experience. The testing phase involved collecting feedback to refine and improve the interactive product.

Executing and validating the user experience was a continuous process in the development of my portfolio website. Actively involving real users for testing allowed me to understand how they interacted with the site and make improvements accordingly. Documentation of the development process provided stakeholders with insights into the iterative journey, fostering transparency and accountability.

For the planet project, The journey video served as an execution and validation of the project's storyline, ensuring a cohesive and impactful conclusion.

The conclusions drawn from these sub-questions served as the basis for justifying design choices throughout the project. This approach ensured that our solutions were grounded in a thorough understanding of the problem space.
